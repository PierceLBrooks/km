Concept: Damage

Superconcept: Impair

Definition: Damage an object (which does not have to be a living thing) so it becomes worse or less effective. The object can still perform its function, but at a diminished capacity.

Required Relationships:

object: The object that's damaged. The object does not have to be a living thing.

Other Common Relationships:

agent: the entity that caused the damage to occur.
instrument: the thing that was used to damage the object.
by-means-of: the event that caused the damage to occur.

Examples:

  1. John damaged the TV with a bat.
    agent: John
    object: the TV
    instrument: a bat
    2. Comment: The TV becomes worse and less effective, but it is not broken. If the TV becomes broken, then use Break to describe the action.

Neighboring Concepts:

Similar to...But different because...
Impair An impair is a general kind of damage where there is no restriction on the object.
Break A break is a special kind of damage where the object is broken and can no longer perform its function.
Harm A harm is similar to a damage, but the object being harmed is a living thing.
