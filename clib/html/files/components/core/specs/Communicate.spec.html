Concept: Communicate

Superconcept: Action

How to represent Communication
Definition: Communication of Information between two Entities.

Required Relationships:

object: Information being communicated.

Other Common Relationships:

agent: The Entity that is the doing the communicating
recipient: The Entity receiving the Information via the communicate
base: The Message which encodes the Information being communicated

Subevents:

Express: the object, which is the Information, is expressed by the agent
Convey: the object, which is the Information, is conveyed to the recipient
Interpret: the object, which is the Information, is interpreted by the recipient

Subevent Order:

Express followed by Convey, followed by Interpret

Examples:

  1. John told Harry that he won the competition.
    object: John won the competition.
    agent: John
    recipient: Harry
    2. Comment: Harry knows that John has won the competition after the communicate.

Neighboring Concepts:

Similar to...But different because...
Transfer Transfer involves a change of physical possession, while Communicate involves passing on Information
Convey Convey involves moving a Message from one entity to another, while Communicate also involves the expression of information as a message and the interpretation of the message back into information. Convey is one step of Communicate.
Transmit Transmit is the movement of a Signal.
Send Send involves Transfer of a physical object (e.g. an envelope), while Communicate involves passing on Information.
