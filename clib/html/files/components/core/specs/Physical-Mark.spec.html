Concept: Physical-Mark

Superconcept: Piece-of-Substance

Definition: Any mark created as the result of writing, sketching, printing, drawing etc (i.e. the result of Mark). Any persistant physical mark or set of marks created by depositing or applying something acting in the role of a writing substance (e.g. ink, paint, sand, blood) to some kind of writable medium using something acting in the role of writing implement (e.g. pencil, marker, finger, paintbrush, spray paint can, printer).

Required Relationships:

result-of: The Mark event by which a given Physical-Mark was created.

Examples:

  1. A trail of marks was left on the white board after the meeting.
  2. Bob signed the check in ink.

Neighboring Concepts:

Similar to...But different because...
Physical-Document A Physical-Document is like a Physical-Mark in that both are the result of Embody events. However, a Physical-Document also includes the medium (e.g. paper) whereas Physical-Mark is merely the trail left in writing, for example. A Physical-Document has a Physical-Mark as a part.
Message A Message is intangible whereas a Physical-Mark is tangible.
